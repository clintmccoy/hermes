# HYP-P003 — Individual analyst (SLIP) is the right initial GTM entry point

**Status:** Untested

**Confidence Score:** 2

## Statement

We believe that the right initial go-to-market motion for Hermes is
single-seat, individual analyst adoption (SLIP — Single-user, Low-friction,
Individual Purchase) — such that early growth comes from analysts self-serving,
rather than from top-down enterprise sales — and that this bottom-up adoption
eventually creates pull that converts to team and firm-level deals.

## Rationale

ARGUS is typically procured top-down and charged to a firm-level budget. Hermes
can undercut that by making it cheap and fast enough for an individual analyst
to try with a credit card, get value immediately, and champion it internally.
This is the classic PLG wedge: land at the individual level, expand to the
team, then convert to enterprise.

The risk is that CRE underwriting is fundamentally a team or firm activity —
deals involve multiple stakeholders, outputs are shared with seniors and LPs,
and the value of consistent underwriting standards is only realized at the firm
level. If individual analysts can't get internal permission to use an
unendorsed tool on real deals, SLIP adoption may be shallow: signups without
actual deal completion, and churning before expansion.

## How to Validate

- Track activation: what % of self-serve signups complete a first real deal
  (not a demo deal) within 30 days?
- Track expansion: what % of individual users attempt to invite a colleague or
  share output within 60 days?
- Ask churned SLIP users: "What prevented you from continuing to use Hermes?"
  — if the answer is "my firm won't let me" or "I need my team on it too," the
  SLIP motion is hitting a structural ceiling.
- Track whether early enterprise leads are analyst-initiated or senior-initiated.
