# HYP-B002 — Senior CRE professionals struggle to train analysts effectively

## Statement

We believe that senior CRE professionals (VPs, Directors, Partners) find it
difficult to make time to train junior analysts on underwriting standards and
deal evaluation — such that there is a real pain point around analyst quality
and consistency that Hermes can address as a co-analyst / training layer.

## Rationale

CRE deal cycles are pressure-filled and time-constrained. Senior professionals
are typically paid to source and close deals, not to teach. Junior analyst
mistakes (wrong assumptions, inconsistent formatting, missed line items) create
rework that falls on seniors. The hidden cost of inconsistent analyst work is
real but hard to measure. If Hermes functions as a consistent, opinionated
co-analyst, it could raise the floor on junior output without requiring senior
time — which is a sellable value proposition at the firm level, not just the
analyst level.

This hypothesis also supports a top-down sales motion alongside the SLIP
bottom-up motion: if the pain is felt most acutely by senior people with
budget authority, they may champion Hermes adoption across their team.

## How to Validate

- Discovery interviews with VPs and above at PE funds, REITs, and developer
  shops. Key question: "How much time do you spend reviewing or correcting
  analyst underwriting? What does it cost you?"
- Ask: "If a junior analyst's first pass was consistently at 80% of your
  standard, what would that be worth to you?"
- Track whether enterprise/team deals at Hermes are initiated by seniors or
  analysts — if seniors are pushing it down, this hypothesis is operative.
