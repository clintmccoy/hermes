## Statement

We believe that CRE investment analysts are broadly dissatisfied with ARGUS as
their primary underwriting tool, and would actively switch to a better
alternative if one existed — such that there is a large, addressable, and
motivated market for Hermes.

## Rationale

ARGUS Enterprise is expensive (often $3,000–$5,000/seat/year), slow to learn,
clunky in UX, and hasn't materially innovated its core product in years. It is
widely described by practitioners as the tool everyone uses because everyone
else uses it — a coordination mechanism, not a loved product. Anecdotal
complaints are abundant in CRE forums, LinkedIn, and practitioner conversations.
The market has a gap: no credible modern alternative has displaced ARGUS at
scale, which suggests the wedge exists but is hard to execute.

The risk is that practitioners tolerate ARGUS not because there's no better
option, but because the switching cost is structural — output formats, LP
expectations, lender templates, and IC processes are all built around ARGUS
conventions. Dissatisfaction may not translate to switching behavior.

## How to Validate

- Structured discovery interviews with 20+ active CRE analysts across firm sizes
  and asset classes. Key question: "If you could replace ARGUS tomorrow with no
  switching cost, would you?" and "What would have to be true for you to switch?"
- Track willingness-to-try in early beta: do analysts sign up and complete a
  first analysis without being hand-held?
- Monitor churn reason at first drop-off: are users leaving because Hermes is
  worse, or because inertia/process won't allow them to switch?
