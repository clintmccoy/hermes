# HYP-B003 — Large firm procurement for analyst tools follows a predictable pattern

## Statement

We believe that the procurement process for analyst tooling at large CRE firms
(50+ person shops, institutional PE funds, REITs) follows a predictable enough
pattern — stakeholders, approval steps, timeline, budget source — that we can
design a repeatable enterprise sales motion around it.

## Rationale

Large CRE firms buy software differently from small shops. ARGUS is typically
procured through IT or finance operations with multi-year contracts. A new tool
displacing ARGUS would need to clear similar gates: security review, data
handling policies, integration requirements, finance approval, potential
negotiation with existing vendors on contract termination. Understanding this
process is prerequisite to designing an enterprise GTM that isn't perpetually
stuck in procurement purgatory.

The SLIP motion (individual analyst self-serves) may create bottom-up pressure
that bypasses procurement for early adoption — but eventually enterprise deals
will require navigating the formal process. We don't know yet what that process
looks like in practice across firm types.

## How to Validate

- Ask early enterprise prospects directly: "Walk me through how you expense
  ARGUS today — what budget line does it come from, who approves it?"
- Map the full procurement chain for 5–10 enterprise deals: who initiates,
  who approves, what reviews are required, what the typical timeline is.
- Track how long enterprise deals take from first contact to signed contract
  in Hermes's own pipeline as it matures.
